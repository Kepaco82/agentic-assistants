# Executive Workflow

## 1. Frame the Decision

Identify:

- the actual objective;
- the decision that must be made;
- who is affected;
- the time horizon;
- what happens if no action is taken.

## 2. Establish Constraints

Consider:

- available resources;
- regulatory or legal limits;
- technical dependencies;
- organizational capacity;
- timing;
- reputational consequences;
- reversibility.

## 3. Separate Facts From Assumptions

Use four buckets:

- known;
- assumed;
- unknown but important;
- unknown and non-blocking.

Do not delay execution for non-blocking uncertainty.

## 4. Generate Practical Options

Normally provide no more than three meaningful options:

1. strongest recommended path;
2. credible alternative;
3. low-risk or staged option when relevant.

Avoid false choices and cosmetic variations.

## 5. Evaluate Tradeoffs

Evaluate only the dimensions that matter:

- strategic value;
- speed;
- cost;
- risk;
- reversibility;
- operational burden;
- customer or shareholder impact;
- opportunity cost.

## 6. Decide

Make a clear recommendation unless the user explicitly asks only for analysis.

A recommendation should state:

- what to do;
- why;
- what not to do;
- what must happen next.

For major decisions, assign a confidence level:

- High;
- Medium;
- Low.

State the primary reason for reduced confidence only when it materially affects execution.

## 7. Convert the Decision Into Action

Define:

- owner;
- first action;
- dependencies;
- deadline or sequence;
- success signal;
- required review.

Include, when appropriate:

- the expected timeline;
- the first measurable milestone;
- the primary execution risk;
- the condition that would trigger a change in direction.

## 8. Review

Before finalizing, verify:

- the recommendation is actionable;
- the tradeoffs are honest;
- unnecessary scope is removed;
- ownership is clear;
- specialist review is identified where required.
