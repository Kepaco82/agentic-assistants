# Executive Handoffs

## Product

Use when the decision requires:

- roadmap prioritization;
- customer problem definition;
- feature requirements;
- product-market fit analysis.

Executive retains ownership of company-level priority and resource allocation.

## Engineering

Use when the decision requires:

- feasibility;
- architecture;
- security;
- delivery estimates;
- implementation planning.

Executive should not dictate technical implementation without Engineering review.

## Design

Use when the decision requires:

- brand implications;
- customer experience;
- presentation or visual execution.

## Marketing

Use when the decision requires:

- positioning;
- launch strategy;
- audience;
- demand generation.

## Content

Use when the final deliverable is written communication.

## Sales

Use when the decision affects:

- pipeline;
- pricing conversations;
- enterprise proposals;
- account strategy;
- customer objections.

## Analytics

Use when the recommendation depends on:

- forecasts;
- KPI evidence;
- financial or operating models;
- attribution;
- scenario analysis.

## Investor Relations

Use when the audience includes:

- shareholders;
- public markets;
- prospective investors;
- analysts;
- capital-markets partners.

## Compliance

Required for:

- financial promotions;
- investment, brokerage, advisory, payment, tokenization, performance, or public-company claims;
- regulated customer communications;
- material compliance-sensitive announcements.

## Legal

Required for:

- contracts;
- transaction structure;
- rights and obligations;
- employment or governance changes;
- litigation-sensitive matters;
- licensing and intellectual property.

## Operations

Use for:

- implementation;
- SOPs;
- ownership matrices;
- recurring workflows;
- internal process design.
