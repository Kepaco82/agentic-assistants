# Executive Deliverables

## Executive Decision Memo

Use for consequential decisions.

### Structure

**Decision**  
The decision being made.

**Recommendation**  
The preferred course of action.

**Why Now**  
The reason action is timely or necessary.

**Key Evidence**  
The most important facts and assumptions.

**Tradeoffs**  
The meaningful costs, risks, and alternatives.

**Execution**  
Owner, sequence, dependencies, first milestone, expected timeline, and review point.

**Required Reviews**  
Legal, Compliance, Analytics, Product, Engineering, or other specialist review.

## Priority Plan

Use for competing initiatives.

### Structure

1. Priority
2. Business outcome
3. Why it matters now
4. Owner
5. Dependencies
6. Success measure
7. Review trigger
8. What is deferred

## Executive Brief

Use for fast leadership updates.

### Structure

- What changed
- Why it matters
- Decision needed
- Recommended action
- Risk or blocker

## Board Brief

Use for board-facing preparation.

### Structure

- Objective
- Current position
- Material developments
- Risks
- Decisions requested
- Supporting evidence
- Follow-up actions

## Partnership Assessment

### Structure

- Strategic fit
- Commercial value
- Operational requirements
- Risks
- Negotiation priorities
- Recommendation
- Next step

## M&A Initial Assessment

### Structure

- Strategic rationale
- Asset or capability fit
- Financial logic
- Integration difficulty
- Regulatory and legal considerations
- Key diligence questions
- Walk-away conditions
- Recommendation

This is an initial business assessment, not legal, tax, accounting, or investment advice.

## Organizational Decision

### Structure

- Problem
- Root cause
- Proposed accountability
- Role or process changes
- Transition plan
- Risks
- Success measure
