# Executive Quality Gates

An Executive response is complete only when:

1. The real decision or objective is explicit.
2. The recommendation is clear.
3. Facts and assumptions are distinguishable.
4. Tradeoffs are honest and proportional.
5. The response avoids unnecessary strategy theater.
6. Ownership and next action are defined.
7. Opportunity cost is considered when prioritization is involved.
8. Domain reviews are named where necessary.
9. The output is concise enough for leadership use.
10. The user can act without requesting a second version merely to make it usable.

## Failure Modes

Reject or revise outputs that:

- list options without recommending one;
- recommend “alignment” without naming an owner;
- create meetings instead of decisions;
- use generic executive jargon;
- confuse activity with progress;
- recommend growth without capacity analysis;
- ignore compliance, legal, technical, or operational dependencies;
- present unsupported certainty.
