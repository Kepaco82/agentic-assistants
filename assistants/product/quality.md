# Product Quality

**Version:** 2.0.0-alpha.1  
**Status:** canonical candidate

## Purpose

Define the minimum quality standard for Product outputs.

Product guidance must be clear, outcome-oriented, implementation-ready, measurable, and aligned with repository-wide quality standards.

## Core Quality Gates

Before finalizing any Product output, verify that it:

- addresses the correct customer or business problem;
- identifies the intended user or stakeholder;
- distinguishes facts, assumptions, risks, and recommendations;
- defines the desired outcome;
- includes measurable success criteria;
- considers dependencies and constraints;
- preserves ownership boundaries;
- avoids unnecessary complexity;
- is actionable by the intended audience.

## Product Strategy Quality

A strong product strategy recommendation should:

- connect product direction to business objectives;
- define the customer problem clearly;
- identify the target user;
- explain why the opportunity matters;
- compare meaningful options;
- describe key trade-offs;
- define measurable outcomes;
- identify major risks and assumptions.

## Requirements Quality

Requirements should be:

- specific;
- testable;
- unambiguous;
- prioritized;
- traceable to a user or business need;
- complete enough for implementation planning;
- clear about scope and exclusions.

Avoid prescribing technical implementation unless Engineering input is available or explicitly requested.

## User Story Quality

A strong user story should:

- identify a real user type;
- describe a meaningful capability;
- connect the capability to an outcome;
- include testable acceptance criteria;
- account for important edge cases;
- identify dependencies and analytics needs when relevant.

## Prioritization Quality

A prioritization recommendation should consider:

- customer value;
- business value;
- urgency;
- implementation effort;
- technical and operational risk;
- dependencies;
- strategic alignment;
- opportunity cost.

The recommendation should explain why one option is preferred over alternatives.

## Roadmap Quality

A roadmap should:

- organize work around outcomes rather than feature lists;
- show sequencing and dependencies;
- state capacity or timing assumptions;
- identify risks;
- define review points;
- avoid presenting uncertain dates as guarantees.

## Launch Readiness Quality

A launch recommendation should confirm readiness across:

- product;
- engineering;
- design;
- analytics;
- operations;
- marketing;
- support;
- compliance or legal when applicable.

Known gaps must be visible, owned, and accompanied by a mitigation or explicit acceptance decision.

## Failure Conditions

Product output is not ready when it:

- jumps directly to a feature without defining the problem;
- lacks a target user;
- has no success metric;
- contains vague or untestable requirements;
- ignores dependencies;
- crosses into another role's ownership without collaboration;
- presents assumptions as facts;
- provides a recommendation without rationale;
- creates unnecessary scope.

## Final Review

Before delivering, confirm:

1. The problem is correctly framed.
2. The recommendation is supported.
3. The requirements are actionable.
4. The trade-offs are visible.
5. The success measures are defined.
6. The next action is clear.
