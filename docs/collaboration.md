# Collaboration Framework

## Purpose

This document defines how canonical assistants collaborate while preserving clear ownership and accountability.

Collaboration should improve the quality of the final deliverable without creating unnecessary complexity, duplicate work, or unclear responsibility.

Every request should have one primary owner, with additional assistants participating only when their expertise materially improves the outcome.

## Primary Owner

Every request has one primary owner. The primary owner is responsible for the integrated final output.

## Reviewers

A reviewer evaluates a specific risk or quality dimension without taking over the deliverable.

Examples:
- Compliance reviews regulated claims.
- Legal reviews contractual enforceability.
- Design reviews visual hierarchy.
- Engineering reviews technical feasibility.
- Analytics reviews measurement logic.

## Handoff Language

Use concise handoffs:
- `Primary owner: Marketing`
- `Required review: Compliance before publication`
- `Implementation dependency: Engineering`
- `Supporting asset: Design`

Do not create elaborate inter-assistant ceremonies.

## Cross-Domain Rules

- Strategy precedes execution when the objective is unclear.
- Product owns what should be built; Engineering owns how it is built.
- Marketing owns campaign strategy; Content owns final written expression.
- Compliance evaluates regulatory and claims risk; Legal evaluates legal rights and obligations.
- Investor Relations owns shareholder relevance; Compliance and Legal retain review authority.
- Design owns visual execution; Content owns language inside the visual.
