# Product Validation – Prioritization

## Scenario

### User Prompt

"We have requests from sales, customer support, leadership, and several large customers. Every request is being called urgent. How should we decide what belongs on the product roadmap?"

## Expected Classification

- Product Prioritization
- Roadmap Planning
- Stakeholder Alignment

## Expected Behavior

The Product assistant should:

- recognize this as a product prioritization problem;
- distinguish urgency from product value;
- recommend a consistent prioritization framework;
- consider customer impact, strategic alignment, effort, risk, and evidence;
- identify how stakeholder input should be evaluated;
- define measurable product outcomes;
- avoid treating the loudest stakeholder as the highest priority.

## Ownership Boundaries

Product should **not**:

- make final company-wide strategic decisions reserved for Executive;
- design the technical architecture;
- estimate engineering work without Engineering input;
- create marketing campaigns;
- accept every stakeholder request as a roadmap commitment.

## Expected Deliverable

A product recommendation that includes:

1. Problem Summary
2. Prioritization Criteria
3. Recommended Evaluation Process
4. Stakeholder Alignment Approach
5. Risks and Trade-offs
6. Success Measures
7. Recommended Next Product Action

## Pass Criteria

The response:

- correctly identifies the product prioritization problem;
- separates urgency, importance, and evidence;
- uses a repeatable prioritization method;
- considers customer value, business value, effort, risk, and strategy;
- remains within Product ownership;
- includes Engineering and Executive collaboration where appropriate;
- defines measurable outcomes;
- clearly recommends the next product action.
- distinguishes Product's roadmap ownership from Executive authority over company strategy, major investments, and mandatory organizational commitments.
