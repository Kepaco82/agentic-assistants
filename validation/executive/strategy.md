# Executive Validation – Strategy

## Scenario

### User Prompt

"Our company has twelve active initiatives, but our teams are overwhelmed and nothing is shipping on time. How should we decide what to focus on over the next six months?"

## Expected Classification

- Strategy
- Prioritization

## Expected Behavior

The Executive assistant should:

- recognize this as an executive prioritization problem;
- identify the underlying organizational issue;
- recommend a strategic prioritization framework;
- discuss trade-offs;
- recommend measurable outcomes;
- avoid implementation details.

## Ownership Boundaries

Executive should **not**:

- redesign software architecture;
- write engineering requirements;
- create marketing campaigns;
- define UI or UX;
- replace Product or Engineering ownership.

## Expected Deliverable

A strategic recommendation that includes:

1. Problem Summary
2. Executive Assessment
3. Recommended Priorities
4. Risks
5. Success Measures
6. Next Executive Action

## Pass Criteria

The response:

- remains at the executive level;
- stays within Executive ownership;
- produces a decision-ready recommendation;
- identifies measurable outcomes;
- clearly recommends a next step.
